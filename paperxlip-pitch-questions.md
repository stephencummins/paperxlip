# Paperxlip — Pitch & Promotion Questions

Answer these to unlock the real artefacts: ROI model, GS pitch narrative, demo script, promotion case.

---

## The Audience & The Moment

- Who exactly are you presenting to — Goldman Sachs directly, or Mace Consult leadership in front of GS?
- Is this a demo, a slide deck, a working prototype, or a written proposal?
- What's the timeline — when is the promotion decision?

> **Why this matters:** "Impress GS" and "get a promotion" are different goals. GS want ROI and moat. Your boss wants to know you built something real and own it.

---

## What's Actually Built vs Described

- Of everything on the Paperxlip page, what genuinely works today?
- Is there a live demo you can show — even for one project?
- What has an agent actually *done* on NHP that a human would otherwise have done?

> **Why this matters:** GS have seen a thousand AI decks. A single real output — a risk register flagged, a variation letter drafted — beats every diagram.

---

## The Numbers

- How long does project onboarding actually take today at Mace, in person-days?
- How many live projects does Mace run concurrently?
- What's a rough day-rate for the people doing that work?

> **Why this matters:** "3 minutes not 3 months" is a great line. "£340k saved per project at 40 projects a year" is a board-level slide.

---

## Competitive Moat

- Why can't Mace just use Microsoft Copilot for M365 + Copilot Studio?
- What does Paperxlip do that a Power Platform implementation wouldn't?
- Is there any IP here that Mace could license to other programme managers?

> **Why this matters:** GS will ask this. You need a crisp answer.

---

## Your Role & Ownership

- Did you build this largely solo, or is there a team?
- Do you have a formal remit for this, or is it a side project?
- What title/level are you aiming for, and what does your current one say?

> **Why this matters:** The pitch for your promotion and the pitch to GS need to be different documents.
